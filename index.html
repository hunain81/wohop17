Our Heritage

A Story Told
In Every Bowl

Tucked beneath the sidewalks of 17 Mott Street, Wo Hop has been feeding New Yorkers since 1938. What began as a humble Cantonese kitchen became a multigenerational institution — a place where cab drivers, artists, judges, and celebrities share the same small tables and the same extraordinary food.

Now in its third generation under the Huang family, guided today by David Leung, Wo Hop earned the James Beard Foundation's America's Classic Award in 2022 — culinary America's highest honor for restaurants that embody the character of their communities.

The menu hasn't changed much since the 1940s, and that's exactly the point. Some things shouldn't.
